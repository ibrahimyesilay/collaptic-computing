# Logo Concept

The mark for Collaptics depicts the single idea the framework is built on: a broad probability field **collapsing** inward toward a point — the Collapse Singularity — through a ring that marks the Collapse Event Horizon.

A vector draft is provided alongside this note: [`logo.svg`](logo.svg).

## Visual idea

```
        . - - - - - .            faint outer rings  = the broad informational field
      .   . - - - .   .          (high entropy, many candidate solutions)
     .   .  ( o )  .   .          tightening rings   = informational collapse
      .   . - - - .   .           dashed ring        = the Collapse Event Horizon
        . - - - - - .             bright core        = the Collapse Singularity (the answer)
```

Concentric rings tighten from the edge toward the centre, representing probability flowing down the potential. A dashed circle near the centre marks the event horizon — the point of no return. The luminous core is the singularity. The wordmark reads **Collaptics**.

## Principles

- **Restraint over spectacle.** The framework is theoretical and academic in tone; the mark should read as a research emblem, not a product badge.
- **One concept, legibly.** Collapse toward a singularity is the whole story; the logo shows exactly that and nothing more.
- **Monochrome-safe.** It must remain clear in a single ink colour and at small sizes (favicon, paper corner).

## Palette (suggested)

- Deep background: near-black blue (`#0b0f14`)
- Field rings: muted cyan (`#3da7c9`)
- Event horizon and core highlight: bright cyan / near-white (`#7fe7ff`, `#eafcff`)

These are suggestions, not requirements. The concept survives any restrained palette.
