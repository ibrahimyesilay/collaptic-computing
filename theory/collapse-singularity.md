# The Collapse Singularity

*The deepest attractor within an informational topology — the answer itself.*

At the center of every problem lies a special structure: the **Collapse Singularity**, the dominant minimum of the informational potential `V(x)`. Its role is analogous to a black hole.

A black hole does not calculate where matter should go. It bends spacetime so that matter naturally falls inward. Likewise, the strongest solution becomes the deepest informational well; probability flows toward it; the solution emerges.

## The hierarchy

A singularity does not stand alone. Collapse descends through four nested structures:

```
Information Field  →  Attractor Basin  →  Collapse Event Horizon  →  Collapse Singularity
   (the cloud)         (drains inward)      (point of no return)         (the answer)
```

- The **field** is the broad, high-entropy cloud of candidate solutions.
- A **basin** is a region that drains toward one answer (see [`informational-gravity.md`](informational-gravity.md)).
- The **event horizon** is where collapse becomes irreversible (see [`collapse-event-horizon.md`](collapse-event-horizon.md)).
- The **singularity** is the bottom of the well — the emergent solution.

## What makes it "the" answer

A singularity is not merely deep; it is the attractor of largest **basin pull** `Γ_k` (depth × width). Multiple singularities can coexist in one substrate. The number of stable singularities per unit volume — **Singularity Density** — is one of the two performance metrics Collaptics proposes in place of FLOPS.

## Honest boundary

The framework resolves the *dominant reachable* singularity, not necessarily the *global* optimum. If barriers between basins are high, the field can be trapped in a sub-optimal well (confirmed: test P5b). Collaptics is not a free solver of hard optimization problems — see [`../examples/hash-inversion.md`](../examples/hash-inversion.md).
