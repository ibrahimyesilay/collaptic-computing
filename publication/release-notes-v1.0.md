## Summary

This is the first public release of Collaptics: a complete, self-contained statement of the framework, its mathematics, its proposed physical substrate, worked examples, and a set of reproducible numerical checks. The release is intended as a stable, citable, archival reference.

## What is included

**Framework and theory**
- A single constitutive relation, `Σ = I · Ω²` (`Ω = ρ·ω`), with a full derivation from one stochastic axiom (the Collapse Flow).
- Core concepts: Collapton, informational field, informational gravity, attractor basins, the Collapse Event Horizon, and the Collapse Singularity, with the collapse hierarchy connecting them.
- Mathematical foundations relating each claim to established results (free-energy/JKO, Kramers timing, basin selection by Laplace, Fisher–Rao information geometry, an embedding of standard methods).

**Hardware**
- A three-layer substrate model — persistent collaptic, resonance dynamics, and photonic interaction layers — described in terms of demonstrated materials and devices.
- A separate, clearly-labelled treatment of speculative materials.

**Examples**
- AI inference, protein folding, optimization, and robotics as candidate framings.
- An honest negative result (cryptographic hash inversion) showing where the framework offers no advantage.

**Simulation**
- A pure-Python (numpy-only), deterministic simulation of the Collapse Flow.
- Six predictions reproduced as assertions: inverse-square sharpening (P1), coherence–temperature equivalence (P2), the Cramér–Rao precision ceiling (P3), basin selection (P5a), kinetic trapping (P5b), and Arrhenius timing (P6).

**Bilingual**
- English is the authoritative edition. A Turkish edition is provided in `tr/` and `whitepaper/Collaptic_Computing_Whitepaper_TR_v1.0.md`.

## Scope and intent

Collaptics is released for open discussion and long-term preservation. The numerical results demonstrate the framework's internal mathematical consistency; a physical machine has not yet been built. Readers are encouraged to test, challenge, and falsify the predictions.

## Honest boundaries

See the dedicated section in [`../README.md`](../README.md). In short: Collaptics is a theoretical framework; it is not a physical device, not experimentally validated, not a quantum computer, and not a universal solver. Its advantage is expected only on smooth, traversable problem landscapes.
